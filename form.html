User Interface Principles used.

The structure principle: The design organizes the user interface purposefully, in meaningful and useful ways based on clear, consistent models that are apparent and recognizable to users, putting related things together and separating unrelated things, differentiating dissimilar things and making similar things resemble one another.
The simplicity principle: The design used has ensured simple and clear communication to the user.
The visibility principle: The design has made all needed options and materials for a given task visible without distracting the user with extraneous or redundant information.
The feedback principle: The design also keeps users informed of actions or interpretations.
The tolerance principle: The design is flexible and tolerant, reducing the cost of mistakes and misuse.
The reuse principle: The design reuses internal and external components and behaviors, maintaining consistency with purpose rather than merely arbitrary consistency, thus reducing the need for users to rethink and remember.
